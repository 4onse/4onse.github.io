Step-down converter preparation¶

  1. Follow this guide (Buck converter soldering) to prepare the buck converters
  2. Follow this guide (Buck converter calibration) to configure the buck converters

Step by step guide¶

  1. Cut the board with the design measures (210 mm x 250 mm)

  2. Print the holes schema and cut appropriately

  3. Stick the hole schema to the plastic board

  4. Verify that the holes fit with your items (connectors, microprocessors and other components)

  5. Eventually, in case of inconsistencies, make appropriate corrections using a colored mark

  6. Drill the signed holes with the correct bit (3M & 2M)

    Note

    Prepare the screws and the spacers

  7. Mount the M3 spacers for the Arduino, RTC, LM393 and the two buck converter on the back part of the plexi

  8. Mount the M2 spacers for the SIM800 and DHT11

  9. Mount the barrier connector with the right screws (M2.5)

  10. Mount the internal components: Arduino, GPRS, OpenLog, RTC, DHT11 and LM393

    Front Back

  11. Follow the schema in the figures below to wire the cables for the power supply system.

    Note

    Please, at this step, do not plug the power cables to the hardware components

    Front Back

  12. Mount the buck converters and plug them

  13. Check that everything is disconneted apart of the buck converter

  14. Check that the right voltage reaches each connector. In the figure below, we checked the Arduino input current.

  15. When you are more than sure that the power circuit is ok, disconnect the main current source and plug the power cables to the components

  16. Following the above steps, the mid-result should be similar to this situation:

    Front Back

    Note

    We used some cable ties to better fix the red and black cables

    Pay attetion: * to the Arduino PIN number where the jumpers must be plugged * to the colors of the cables
